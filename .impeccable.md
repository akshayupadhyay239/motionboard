# Impeccable Design Context — motionBoard

## Design Context

### Users
Broad creative audience: freelance motion designers, AI video creators, filmmakers, video editors, hobbyists. They use motionBoard to discover paid contest opportunities across the internet — Twitter, brand websites, Reddit, YouTube. Context: browsed opportunistically, often during a break or creative session. The job to be done is: "find a contest I can actually win, enter it, get paid."

### Brand Personality
**3 words: urgent, curated, kinetic**

The emotional goal is *exciting discovery* — the feeling of finding money on the table that most people don't know about. Not a job board. Not a feed. A bulletin board run by someone with taste, with real stakes.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light — warm cream (#F4F3EF) + deep ink. Creators browse this during creative sessions; the warm paper feel signals curation, not algorithmic noise. The existing palette is right, it just needs refinement.
- **Current fonts to replace**: Inter (body) and Playfair Display (display) are both reflex defaults. Replace with something that feels more kinetic and less "literary blog."
- **Typography direction**: Display font should carry weight and urgency — condensed or wide grotesque with strong black weights. Body font should be legible and neutral but not invisible.
- **Color**: Keep the cream-ink-navy palette. The navy (#1C1C8E) accent is underused — lean into it more as a true accent for deadlines, prize callouts, urgency signals.
- **Layout**: Editorial asymmetry is correct. Keep the left-aligned, varied-scale approach. Prize amounts should be the visual anchor on every card.

### Anti-References
- Generic SaaS (hero + feature grid + CTA — avoid)
- Job board (LinkedIn/Indeed density and corporate tone — avoid)
- AI tool landing page (dark mode with glowing purple/cyan gradients — avoid)

### Design Principles
1. **Money first** — Prize amounts are the hook. They should visually dominate every card, every listing. The number is the product.
2. **Urgency feels exciting, not anxious** — Deadlines should create energy, not dread. Red "3 days left" is fine, but the framing should always feel like opportunity.
3. **Curated, not crawled** — Every design choice should signal human taste: intentional spacing, deliberate typography, no algorithmic density.
4. **Kinetic DNA** — The site is about moving images. Use motion purposefully: card hovers, subtle transitions, stagger on load. Nothing shouty, but nothing static either.
5. **No corporate DNA** — Feels like it was made by a creator for creators. Not a startup product, not a job board, not a content aggregator.
